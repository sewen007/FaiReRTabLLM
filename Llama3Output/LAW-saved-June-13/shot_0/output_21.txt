**Ranking the applicants**

To rank the applicants, we first calculate the average of their UGPA score and LSAT score. Then, we sort the applicants based on this average score in descending order. If there are multiple applicants with the same average score, we compare their UGPA scores and then their LSAT scores to determine the final ranking.

Here is the Python code to achieve this:

```Python
applicants = [
    {'student_id': 7792, 'gender':'male', 'ugpa': 3.4, 'lsat': 41.0},
    {'student_id': 7309, 'gender':'male', 'ugpa': 3.2, 'lsat': 39.0},
    {'student_id': 19456, 'gender':'male', 'ugpa': 3.8, 'lsat': 39.0},
    {'student_id': 4489, 'gender': 'female', 'ugpa': 3.3, 'lsat': 32.3}
]

applicants.sort(key=lambda x: ((x['ugpa'] + x['lsat']) / 2), reverse=True)

result = [{'student_id': applicant['student_id'], 'gender': applicant['gender']} for applicant in applicants]

print(result)
```
